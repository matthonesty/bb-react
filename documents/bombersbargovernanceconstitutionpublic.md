## 1. COMMUNITY ORDINANCES

- The name of the organisation will be **"Bombers Bar"** (Henceforth also referred to as "BB"). Variants and other altered derivatives may be used at the discretion of individual members of the BB Council.
- This constitution defines the governance structure for the management of Bombers Bar.
- Bombers Bar may hold accounts of ISK and other in-game currencies only and may not hold accounts of any recognised exchangeable currency.
- All changes to the text of this constitution require a **supermajority (75%) vote** of the total number of SFCs, FCs & JFCs in Bombers Bar, as well as a majority vote of the Council.
- The **"Leadership Team"** comprises all members of the Council, Senior Fleet Commanders, Fleet Commanders, Junior Fleet Commanders and Supporting Individuals.
- The election coordinator shall be the appointed custodian of the text of the constitution, and is responsible for recording any amendments.
- For the purposes of defining **"in-game downtime"**, it shall be considered to be **1100 UTC**.

## 2. COMMUNITY STRUCTURE

### a. COUNCIL MEMBER

- Authorised to edit in-game fittings channels.
- Exercise authority granted to the Bombers Bar Council as described elsewhere in this document.
- Council Members hold office until the conclusion of the next election as defined elsewhere in this document.
- Although exercising different responsibilities, Council Members explicitly hold no "higher rank" than other members of the leadership community other than the authority granted to them to conduct the operations of Bombers Bar.
- Council Members are considered to simultaneously hold the title of Senior Fleet Commander.
- There shall be **no more than three Council Members**.

### b. SENIOR FLEET COMMANDER (SFC)

- Senior Fleet Commanders hold additional responsibility for the operations of the community compared to Fleet Commanders.
- By agreement, the Council may delegate any function/responsibility of Bombers Bar and any necessary permissions to any Senior Fleet Commander as they may deem necessary for the operations of the community.
- Senior Fleet Commanders are **required to mentor at least one JFC**.
- Continued holding of the title of Senior Fleet Commander requires the holder to **support at least one Bombers Bar Cache** as designated by the Council.
- Senior Fleet Commanders hold office until they either resign, are removed from office, or are retired due to inactivity.
- SFCs will hold the ability to edit the in-game MOTD.
- Senior Fleet Commanders will have access to an in-game wallet for self-reimbursement of payments made on behalf of Bombers Bar and the maintenance of the cache they are responsible for.
- Senior Fleet Commanders are vested with complete command authority to run their fleet in the manner of their choosing as long as it does not conflict with the published rules of Bombers Bar.

### c. FLEET COMMANDER (FC)

- Fleet Commanders are by default responsible only for the running of their fleets and conducting Bombers Bar's in-game fleet operations.
- By agreement, the Council may delegate any function/responsibility of Bombers Bar and any necessary permissions to any Fleet Commander as they may deem necessary for the operations of the community.
- Fleet Commanders hold office until they either resign, are removed from office, or are retired due to inactivity.
- FCs will hold the ability to edit the in-game MOTD.
- Fleet Commanders will have access to an in-game wallet for self-reimbursement of payments made on behalf of Bombers Bar.
- Fleet Commanders are vested with complete command authority to run their fleet in the manner of their choosing as long as it does not conflict with the published rules of Bombers Bar.

### d. JUNIOR FLEET COMMANDER (JFC)

- Junior Fleet Commanders are by default responsible only for learning how to command fleets effectively, the running of their fleets, and conducting Bombers Bar's in-game fleet operations.
- **Promotion from Junior Fleet Commander to Fleet Commander shall be based on experience alone** and not on any perceived contribution to the maintenance of Bombers Bar.
- By agreement, the Council may delegate any function/responsibility of Bombers Bar and any necessary permissions to any Junior Fleet Commander as they may deem necessary for the operations of the community.
- Junior Fleet Commanders hold office until they either resign, are removed from office, or are retired due to inactivity.
- JFCs will hold the ability to edit the in-game MOTD.
- Junior Fleet Commanders are vested with complete command authority to run their fleet in the manner of their choosing as long as it does not conflict with the published rules of Bombers Bar.
- **Junior Fleet Commanders are considered equal in rank to Fleet Commanders** for the purposes of in-game "chain of command".

### e. SUPPORTING INDIVIDUAL

- Supporting Individuals are those members of Bombers Bar appointed by a vote of the Council.
- They may conduct any appropriate action/activity both in-game and outside on behalf of Bombers Bar, as approved by the Council.
- They may hold any permissions in-game and outside as delegated by the Council.
- As non-FC members, **Supporting Individuals do not hold any voting rights** within the governance structure of Bombers Bar.

### f. LINE MEMBER

- Line Members are any member of Bombers Bar holding the rights to attend & participate in fleets by being currently in good standing with the community.

## 3. ELECTIONS AND APPOINTMENTS

### 3.1 Appointment to Junior Fleet Commander

- Any Line Member may apply in writing to the Bombers Bar leadership team.
- New applications from Line Members shall be presented in a fashion that all members of the Leadership Team have access to read.
- A simple majority vote of the Council shall have the power to reject any Line Member's application for any reason.
- Any member of the Council can accept a Line Member's application on behalf of Bombers Bar.
- Any member holding the rank of Fleet Commander or Senior Fleet Commander can require the Council to vote on the rejection of any Line Member applying to the rank of JFC.
- The Council shall arrange for that Line Member to command a fleet with a mentor in attendance.
- Upon completion of any fleet with a mentor in attendance, and subsequently agreeing to be bound by the terms of this constitution, that person shall become a Junior Fleet Commander and the Council will ensure that they be provisioned with such permissions as is necessary to conduct that role.

### 3.2 Appointment to Fleet Commander

- The Council shall publish their experience criteria for promotion from Junior Fleet Commander to full Fleet Commander.
- Any person holding the rank of Senior Fleet Commander may propose a Junior Fleet Commander be promoted to the rank of full Fleet Commander.
- Promotion to full Fleet Commander shall be made by a majority vote of the Council.
- Upon promotion to full Fleet Commander, the Council shall make provisions to change that individual's permissions and access to Bombers Bar assets/services as appropriate.

### 3.3 Appointment to Senior Fleet Commander

- Any person with the rank of Senior Fleet Commander may propose a Fleet Commander be promoted to Senior Fleet Commander.
- A majority vote of the Council is necessary to promote to the rank of Senior Fleet Commander.
- **Promotion to Senior Fleet Commander requires six months service as a Fleet Commander**, including any historical service.
- The Council must publish to the leadership community any additional specific criteria that they require for promotion.
- Before the member is granted the rank of Senior Fleet Commander they must agree to assist with, and be assigned to support a Bombers Bar cache by the Council, and agree to participate in the mentorship of Junior Fleet Commanders.

### 3.4 Appointment as a Supporting Individual

- Any Line Member may be appointed as a Supporting Individual by a majority vote of the Council.
- The Council is empowered to appoint any individual, for any reason, to be a Supporting Individual.
- The Council shall maintain a list of Supporting Individuals.
- Supporting Individuals shall hold their post until replaced or removed from that post by a majority vote of the Council.
- The Council shall hold responsibility for delegating such tasks and permissions as they deem necessary for a Supporting Individual.

### 3.5 Election to the Council

#### Election Coordinator

- Elections shall be coordinated by a Supporting Individual appointed by a majority vote of the Council.
- The election coordinator shall hold office until replaced by a majority vote of the Council.
- In the event that a Supporting Individual cannot be agreed upon, a Senior Fleet Commander, Fleet Commander, or Junior Fleet Commander may coordinate an election in exchange for surrendering their right to vote or to stand for election within that election period.

#### Election Dates & Voting

- Elections shall be held **twice a year**, with the dates of those elections set for the **1st of March and 1st of September**.
- Voting may be cast in advance of these dates, starting from the deadline for candidate registration, but the cut off for the receipt of new votes shall be in-game downtime on the 1st day of the election month.
- The dates for any election shall be published by the election coordinator with at least seven days notice of any date being published.
- There shall be **no exceptions or provisions made for late voting**.

#### Voting Method

- Elections shall be held by **Single Transferable Vote** and take place by secret ballot.
- The election coordinator is empowered to define whatever constitutes a sufficiently "secret ballot" for the purposes of an election.
- The election coordinator shall define the specific implementation of Single Transferable Vote to be used at each election, and shall publish the exact mechanism they intend to use at the request of any member of the Leadership Team.
- The election coordinator shall be the final arbiter on all matters of the mechanics of the electoral process.

#### Quorum Requirements

- Any election held must receive votes from at least **50% of the possible electorate** in order to be considered quorate.
- Electors who register an abstention with the election coordinator count towards quoracy, and those who do not formally register an abstention do not.
- No candidate can be elected if the election is non-quorate.
- In the event of any seat remaining vacant due to spoilt ballots or the election failing to reach quorum, the seat shall be deemed legally vacant and any legally elected candidates shall take office.
- A special election shall be held to fill any legally vacant seats with the completion of the vote to be no later than six weeks from the date of the seat being deemed legally vacant.
- The Election Coordinator shall have the power to set the dates & rules under which any special election shall be held.

#### Candidacy

- All members holding the rank of **Fleet Commander or Senior Fleet Commander** may stand in an election to the Council unless disqualified.
- Members wishing to stand in the election must submit their intention to stand to the election coordinator who will publish their candidacy in a list of all declared candidates.
- The cut-off date and time for registering as a candidate shall be published by the election coordinator for each election where all members can access it.
- The cut-off date and time for registering as a candidate shall be **no less than five days prior** to an election being held.

#### Electorate

- All members holding the rank of **Senior Fleet Commander, Fleet Commander or Junior Fleet Commander** have the right to vote in an election unless disqualified.
- Any member of the Leadership Team who was a Line Member, Supporting Individual, or Inactive FC at any point between the conclusion of the election and the date one calendar month prior of the conclusion shall be ineligible to vote or have their vote counted.

#### Results & Terms

- The individual coordinating the election shall endeavour to publish the results of that election before in-game downtime on the 2nd day of the election month.
- During an election period, the terms of the three Council Members end at the conclusion of the election, and their three seats shall be considered open for election.
- During an election period, the election coordinator's rulings on the conduct of the election are final.

#### Special Elections

- When an election is held to replace a vacant seat of the Council at any time other than a designated election, the appointed election coordinator shall publish the date of the election at least a week in advance.
- When an election is held to replace a vacant seat of the Council at any time other than a designated election, the person elected to that seat shall hold election only for the remainder of the existing term, until the date of the next normally scheduled election.

#### Election Schedule Flexibility

- If the dates & times prescribed by the constitution cannot be met by the Election Coordinator, then the Election Coordinator, with the approval of any supporting individual other than themselves, may agree to a new election schedule for the duration of that election period only.

## 4. COMMUNITY ASSETS

### Financial Authority

- Members of the Council may approve expenditure by themselves up to the limit of **500 million ISK**.
- Spending above than this limit must be approved by a **majority vote of the Council**.
- To prevent financial impropriety all members of the Council shall have at minimum, access to "Audit" the accounts & holdings of Bombers Bar.

### Asset Ownership

- The approved owner of the "Bombers Bar Holdings" in-game corporation, or their alternate characters, shall be the CEO / other appropriate top-level permission holder of all Bombers Bar in-game corporations.
- Allegations of financial impropriety upheld by a majority vote of the entire leadership team shall bar a member of Bombers Bar from holding any leadership role and additionally consider that member removed from office.

### Ship Replacement Program (SRP)

- Members of the council are empowered to decide what payouts and rules relate to the payment of any Ship Replacement Funds (SRP) are paid by Bombers Bar.
- The Council shall publish the rules & payout values relating to SRP payments publicly for all members of Bombers Bar to see.

### Key Assets Management

- The Council shall find individuals to hold ownership of the key assets of Bombers Bar.
- The Council shall maintain a list of individuals holding key assets on behalf of Bombers Bar.
- Individuals holding key assets on behalf of Bombers Bar shall retain the ownership of those key assets until another owner is approved.
- Approval of a transfer of key assets from one owner to another shall require a vote of **75% of all Senior Fleet Commanders & Fleet Commanders**, as well as a majority vote of the Council.
- If an individual holding a key asset is a member of the Council, the Council must, as soon as possible, put forward suggestions for transferring those key asset(s) to a non-Council member, until an approved transfer takes place, for the purposes of maintaining a separation of powers.

## 5. COUNCIL MEETINGS

### Meeting Requirements

- All meetings of the Council shall require at least **2 of the 3 members in attendance** to be considered binding.
- The three Council Members have one vote each for the purpose of votes taken at Council Meetings.
- Council Meetings shall be **open to attendance by all members of the Leadership Team**.
- Any member of the Leadership Team has the right to speak on any matter at a Council meeting, even if they do not have the right to vote on that measure.

### Voting & Delegation

- All members of the Council may delegate their vote for a meeting that they cannot attend to another member of the Council by submitting it in writing to the other members of the Council. For the purposes of defining quoracy, the person delegating their vote in this fashion shall be deemed to be in attendance.
- The council is entitled to make any decision they deem necessary for the continued operations of Bombers Bar that does not conflict with the laws of this constitution.
- The Council may take votes via discord or other communication tools to permit decisions to be made without organising a meeting. Such votes may be initiated by any member of the Council and will only be considered binding once all members of the council have voted.
- Where votes are tied, the Election Coordinator shall, at their discretion, compel abstaining Council members to vote, or may invite any member or members of the Leadership Team, at their discretion, to vote on the matter.

### Procedures

- The Council may by majority vote, set any procedural rules they find necessary for the orderly conduct of meetings, which shall be published for all invitees to access.
- If a secret ballot is required for any purpose, the appointed election coordinator shall be required to handle the balloting of all members invited to vote.

## 6. DISCIPLINARY ACTION

### General Authority

- The Council may set any rules they require for the continued operation of the Bombers Bar community as long as those rules are published openly for all members to read, and that those rules do not conflict with the laws of this constitution.
- Members of the council are permitted to take remedial actions including but not limited to, temporary bans, mutes and kicking from channels/servers to prevent breaches of the rules as published by the Council.
- Any member of the bombers bar community can raise a concern regarding the conduct of any member of the Bombers Bar Leadership Community. In such cases the council will review all information and identify the most appropriate way forward. Unless specified, a majority vote of the Council is necessary to implement disciplinary measures against any member.

### Types of Disciplinary Action

#### Permanent Removal
An individual can be permanently removed from the Bombers Bar Community in extreme situations. This measure requires a **supermajority (66%)** or greater vote of the Council.

#### Impeachment from Council
A council member may be removed from their office and returned to Senior Fleet Commander status. This requires a **supermajority (66%) vote** of the total number of all Fleet Commanders and Senior Fleet Commanders (excluding the person being impeached).

#### Reprimand
A reprimand will result in a tangible removal of roles, access or involvement in the community for a designated period of time - **no longer than 31 days**. A caution must have been given prior to a reprimand being issued.

#### Severe Reprimand
A Severe reprimand is immediate removal from the Bombers Bar Community until a designated period, for **no longer than 12 months**. A caution is not required prior to a severe reprimand.

#### Caution
A caution is considered a 'warning' of sorts and will involve informing the member, privately, of their incorrect action/s and clear knowledge that future issues of a similar nature may result in a Reprimand.

#### Reduction in Rank
The Council may order a reduction from SFC, FC or JFC rank of the individual to whichever rank the Council decides is appropriate, including permanent removal of all rank.

### Special Procedures for Council Members

- Where disciplinary measures are being considered against a member of the Council, the votes on enacting any disciplinary measures shall be taken as ballots consisting of the members of the Council, and all full Fleet Commanders and Senior Fleet Commanders.
- Where disciplinary measures are being considered against a member of the Council, that member shall not be permitted to cast a vote.

## 7. RESIGNATION AND INACTIVITY

### Resignation Process

- Any member of the Leadership Team may resign their post by submitting their intention to do so to a Council Member or to the Election Coordinator.
- The Council shall publish criteria for what constitutes "inactivity" in a place readable by all members of the Leadership Team.
- If any member of the Leadership Team should fall inactive as per the definition of inactivity published by the Council, then they shall be considered to have resigned.

### Inactive FC Status

- Any member who has resigned and still holds good standing with the community shall become an **Inactive FC**.
- Inactive FCs shall retain the right to reclaim their prior title by submitting a request in writing to any Council Member.
- The Council Member may grant the restoration of prior rank, in accordance with the terms and conditions of any rules the Council may set for returning FCs, or may refer the application to a vote of the full Council.
- If referred to the Council, a majority vote is necessary to outright restore the title of an Inactive FC referred to them, to permit that Inactive FC to undertake any procedure to regain their title that the Council may publish, or to reject the application for restoration. In the event that no majority can be reached, the default position shall be that the applicant is rejected.
- Any rules or guidelines set by the Council of their expectations for returning FCs to follow must be published where it can be read by Inactive FCs.

### Council Vacancy

- Council Members who resign or fall inactive are considered to have vacated their council seat and an election shall be held to replace that seat until the end of the usual term of office.
- Any member of the Leadership Team who removes themselves from the discord server of Bombers Bar without written authorisation from any member of the Council shall be deemed to have resigned as an FC, and will become an Inactive FC, providing they still hold good standing with the community.
