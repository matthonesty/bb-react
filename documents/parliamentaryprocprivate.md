# BB Parliamentary Procedure

**Meeting Rules for Council and Leadership Meetings**

---

## Meeting Leadership

### Chairman

- An FC meeting shall have a **Chairman** designated by majority vote of the Council members in attendance.
- The Chairman may be any individual among the leadership team in attendance, including Council members.
- It shall be the job of the meeting Chairman to run the meeting in accordance with these rules of procedure.

### Speaking Time

- There is no defined time that a member is allowed to speak.
- The Chair may direct members to be mindful of their time or to stay on topic during discussions in the interest of progressing the meeting and keeping it on track.

### Secretary

- A **Secretary** shall be designated to record minutes of the discussion and the votes taken during the meeting.
- Minutes shall be recorded upon the meeting agenda document.

---

## Lifecycle of a Standard Agenda-Item Motion

### 1. Introduction

The motion is introduced by the member that either:
- Placed it on the agenda, or
- Is designated as "who" for that item

The motion requires a **second** to be considered. If no second is heard, the motion is not considered.

### 2. Debate Period

Period of debate/discussion of the motion by Council members. Motions to amend the subject of discussion may be made.

The Chair may:
- Allow free discussion, or
- Rotate among members or anyone else designated as a speaker, recognizing them one at a time to speak their mind as appropriate

### 3. Open Discussion

At some point before discussion has concluded, the Council members shall move to direct the Chair to **open the discussion to the rest of the leadership team** in attendance.

### 4. Cloture Vote

Cloture vote of debate period (end debate)

### 5. Final Vote

Vote on the motion
