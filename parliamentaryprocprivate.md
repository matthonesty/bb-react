BB parliamentary procedure:


An FC meeting shall have a Chairman designated by majority vote of the Council members in attendance. The Chairman may be any individual among the leadership team in attendance, including Council members.
It shall be the job of the meeting Chairman to run the meeting in accordance with these rules of procedure.

There is no defined time that a member is allowed to speak. The Chair may direct members to be mindful of their time or to stay on topic during discussions in the interest of progressing the meeting and keeping it on track.

A Secretary shall be designated to record minutes of the discussion and the votes taken during the meeting. Minutes shall be recorded upon the meeting agenda document.




Lifecycle of a standard agenda-item motion:
The motion is introduced by the member that either placed it on the agenda or is designed as “who” for that item. The motion requires a second to be considered. If no second is heard, the motion is not considered.
Period of debate/discussion of the motion by Council members. Motions to amend the subject of discussion may be made.
The Chair may allow free discussion or may rotate among members or anyone else designated as a speaker, recognizing them one at a time to speak their mind as appropriate.
At some point before discussion has concluded, the Council members shall move to direct the Chair to open the discussion to the rest of the leadership team in attendance.
Cloture vote of debate period (end debate)
Vote on the motion
